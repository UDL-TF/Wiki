### Direct
The rocket goes straight towards the target, usually done in one of two ways: aiming at target or dragging towards target. Or as  a result of more advanced methods. The rocket will generally fly faster at a direct path, making it harder to react.
### Curve
When the rocket is aimed off-center from the target with or without flick force, it will curve the rocket's trajectory toward the target. Giving them more time to respond and making the trajectory appear easier to follow with the eye.
#### Iteration
- Note that past a certain mph, a curve can turn into an S shape flight path, and later a Z shape path. Z shape specifically is hard to track as it appears to speed up non-linearly.
- in rare cases, the rocket will curve in an S shape twice before reaching the target, this has colloquially been coined Snake Rocket.
### Orb / Orbit
When The rocket moves past you, either to the side or above / below, orbits around
you in a 360 degree circle back to the front, in order to hit it more predictably and delay the reflection in case it's needed.
#### Iterations
- can be done by either side-stepping the incoming rocket with A or D, then pressing A, S, D, W, A, S, D.... in rapid succession.
- can be done by just pressing the opposite key of the incoming rocket's trajectory, followed by pressing S and quickly tapping the opposite key (a or d) from before, this is useful for high mph
- By jumping above a down spiked rocket, you can start a vertical orbit. Same thing by letting an upspike pass over and behind you and then jump back towards it.
- You can close the distance to your target by moving forward while orbiting, using A, W, D.
### Drag / Dragging
The act of aiming at the rocket and flicking it in a certain direction while reflecting at a perfect timing to change it's trajectory.
#### Iterations
- Regular drags are done by aiming at the rocket and flicking toward desired target. The more accurately aimed and abruptly stopped above target location, the cleaner the drag will be and the ideal L shape will be harder to predict as it increases mph.
- Bounce-dragging can be done by looking at the floor at an angle, then flicking up at the target location and abruptly stopping the mouse. This can be done from the side's (south west / east mouse position or 5 / 7 'o clock) and then dragged toward the target.
- you can drag both upspikes and straight rockets by aiming slightly above and then flicking a very short distance toward the target and coming to a stop, creating a vertical drag. 
### CQC
Close quarter Combat is what fighting at close range is referred to specifically for it's unique playstyle forced upon both participants.
It relies heavily on predicting your enemy and utilizing advanced drag and orbit methods.
### Rally
Rallying is the process of reflecting in a 1v1 — usually — being at a far distance and attempting to just go for as high a speed as possible, generally paying less attention to technique. Though on most servers, it is recommended to not direct the rocket or go for aggressive dragging in this circumstance. Rather, it's easiest for your opponent and you, if you simply reflect in an easy fashion and past a certain mph (usually 300+) start by flicking up ever so slightly, causing a sort of vertical S shape curve. This way the rocket has a longer trajectory, resulting in a very time-consistent back and forth, which helps to achieve very high speeds consistently.
The higher the speed climbs, the more consistent the rocket will behave even with alternations to the trajectory.
#### Iterations
On certain Server configs that utilize a lower "Turn Rate" value at high speeds, it is recommended to move backwards as the rocket comes towards you. This is because a loose turn rate value at high speed can cause the rocket to miss you and simply travel past you (out of direct range) possibly forcing you into an orbit, at a 1000mph+ it is barely possible to do. Beware! that moving backwards when the rocket approaches you in an S curve which does NOT end directly in front of you, will forcibly orbit you. This can be utilized by is not recommended at rally speeds.
- Some servers have limitations beyond high mph values causing the rocket to lag. Avoid those at all cost if you want to rally!

## Walling
Standing still bunched up together as a team of two or more people, everyone hitting around the same time is called Walling, according to some players, this used to be how you bypass collision by standing near a wall.
### FOV
Field of View refers to the ingame fov which can be extended beyond TF2's default max range of 90 on Dodgeball servers. Many players utilize this to get a tighter area to aim at or artificially increase their sensitivity without increasing it in settings. It can also help to have a lower fov to more easily see your opponents actions and aim at them for drag. Minimum value is FOV 20.
Though max FOV differs from server to server, some allowing up to 170.

### FP / TP
- FP refers to first person, the regular way of seeing the game from the perspective of your pyro. In this mode, you generally have a more immersive experience and some players prefer to judge the rocket's trajectory towards them for timing this way.
- TP refers to third person, where you see your player model from above/behind. In this mode, you can see more of the battlefield in all directions. It is useful for cqc combat and some individual players prefer this over FP in all cases. A common occurrence with TP is that you are aiming toward the ground rather than straight ahead, causing downspikes or bounce-drag without effort or intention.

## Rocket Manipulation Methods

### Upspike (US)
When the rocket is dragged upwards, it's trajectory goes in an upside-down U shape toward the target. This can be hard to counter if it's unexpected and the target is not aware of where to place their crosshair to hit it.
#### Iterations:
- if simply aimed upwards at an angle, the rocket takes a conservative curve toward the target, making it possible to hit it frontally.
- if the rocket is dragged all the way to the highest possible viewing angle, the rocket will orbit the player vertically from above.
- if aimed slightly above the target and dragged perfectly onto their head height, sometimes it causes a vertical orbit which starts a trajectory identical to a direct, waving at the end and hitting the targets back, leaving no possibility to orbit.
### Downspike (DS)
Just like upspikes, the rocket is dragged (toward the ground).
Depending on the intensity and amount of the flick, the rocket will proceed to bounce off the floor toward the target. The numbers of bounces can be manipulated here (though server configs can influence this) generally higher intensity = more bounces. As bounces stop the forward momentum of the rocket temporarily, this can be used to trick an opponent into reflecting early. After the bounces end, the rocket will usually fly directly toward the target, suddenly speeding up in momentum.
#### Iteration
- If the rocket is dragged straight down, it creates a more intense bounce with a straight forward trajectory after.
- if the rocket is dragged from left to right or vise versa while downspiking, it creates a Z shape, going forward on one side, then suddenly bouncing over to the other side of the target. This can catch someone off-guard and surprise them.
### Back-Dragging / Backfire (BF)
Back-dragging is done by moving the rocket in a straight 180 degree. Causing the trajectory to temporarily face away from the target and then turning around towards them. This changes the trajectory timing and can be hard to time especially at higher mph.
#### Iterations
- by dragging the rocket exactly 180 degrees behind you, it will either go through/past you and then turn around towards your target or...
- at high speed the rocket will do a regular bf without having to turn around.
- if done very fast, the rocket will turn away from the target in front of you and then face the target again. (Colloquially called front-bf)
- by letting the rocket orbit you half way, you can turn around and simply fire it away from the targets direction. This can be harder to do at high mph and not facing your opponent can be a certain death.
### Delayed Orbit-Drag
in cqc to medium range combat, a straight forward orbit can be very predictable, one solution is to orbit the rocket and when it's in front of you, let it pass by until it is almost entirely back to your sideview, then drag it from the side to the target's location. Their expectation is for you to hit an orbit when the rocket faces you, not the other way around.
#### Iteration
This can of course also done by interrupting the full orbit by turning around and dragging the rocket to the front before it can finish one rotation. Either version breaks the expectation of when you'll go for the reflect.
### Sniping
Rocket Auto Targeting works by selecting the opponent farthest away from you (all things equal) this can of course be manipulated using dragging — or even curves and relies on the server config's "Weighting" parameters.
When two players of the same team stand in front of each other, targeting the player behind with a drag / direct is considered sniping. It's always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings, even your teammates positions. If you see a rocket going towards you and there was no beep to indicate it's yours, chances are someone is behind you.
#### Iterations
Colloquially I've seen sniping also used as in getting really close to the enemy team and upon receiving a rocket, you drag towards a desired target in close range, since the choice by +1 targets appears random for them, they don't really have time to react unless they're aware of you being there. (This can happen if NER is enabled or RTD (invisibility / small pyro)
### Stealing
If you reflect a rocket that is not designated to target you, it is considered stealing. Sometimes you can use this to save a teammate in need or save yourself from being sniped. But beware, a lot of servers consider this method undesired and will punish it in one way or another. (Usually once or twice is ok)
#### Iteration
- If you hug your teammate to team up, you're generally not punished for stealing their rocket.
- If you stand very close to the enemy, you can steal your teammates rocket, though some servers have made it impossible to kill your target by reflecting at 0 range.
### Rocket-Jumping
Upon a vertical orbit at starting mph, the damage scaling has not yet reached lethal, So you can use the explosion hitting you from behind/below to rocket jump. If done right you can travel the entire map distance easily. Can be used strategically to force your opponent into a CQC. And if you jump past the Rocket-Spawn, you can pick up the new rocket from there and reflect it upon landing at close range.
#### Iteration
Some maps allow you to get higher elevation by jumping up on certain spaces in the maps.
They can also be accessed through hidden entrances.
